Japan

Until recently, Japan was the world's third largest economy behind the US and China, but recessions and a declining population have resulted in the country dropping to number four. Regardless, Japan remains a powerhouse. Unlike many other regions in East Asia, Japan was never formally or even informally colonized by a European power. Instead, beginning with the Meiji Restoration in the 1860s, Japan was able to chart a course toward modernization while preserving its own cultural agency. Of course, this eventually led to the establishment of the Japanese Empire and its own conquest and colonization of many parts of East and Southeast Asia and the Pacific until its defeat at the end of World War II in 1945. The presence of post-war US bases and generations of American service members may have played an instrumental role in the implantation and adoption of Western music, notably rock and early heavy metal, which resulted in the creation of the earliest and ultimately the most developed metal scence of the four countries in this study. Notably, Japan has one of the most unique metal scenes of any country in the world and classic and older sub-genres that are no longer very popular in much of the world have preserved their popularity in Japan. The Budokan arena has long been one of the world's most famous metal venues. The current prime minister, Sanae Takaichi, is a metal fan and drummer and is particularly fond of Iron Maiden and recently met with Deep Purple.

Babymetal is a perfect example of the unique nature of the metal scene in Japan. They perform "kawaii" metal which blends Japanese popular music, aka J-pop, with its clean and layered vocals, on-stage dance routines, and idolization of the three singers with a classic heavy metal/power metal sound courtesy of their killer backing band.
