China

China, officially the People's Republic of China, is the world's second most populous country after India with a population of 1.4 billion in 2024 and has consistently been one of the fastest growing economies in the world over the past several decades. A millenia-long history of dyanstic rule came to an end in 1949 with the Communist Revolution and the Chinese Communist Party has ruled the country ever since. Following the end of the turbulent Cultural Revolution from 1966-76, the country launched a series of reforms and opened up to the rest of the world leading to its economic expansion and growing global influence. Questions regarding freedom of expression are undoubtedly an issue that metal bands have to contend with in China.





Example of Distinctive Chinese Metal: Tang Dynasty Interactive Heat Map of Chinese Metal Bands

Established in 1988, Tang Dynasty is widely accepted to be China's first metal band. The band takes its name from the historical Tang Dynasty, 618-907 CE, an expansive empire whose borders extended along the Silk Roads into Central Asia. They are a prime example of how a global music genre can be assimilated into local culture and history. Their lyrical themes center around the historic Chinese conception of the "ideal man" who exhibits both cultural refinement, or "wen," and martial ability, or "wu." The band's lyrical content is largely based on poetry from the Tang period, reflecting "wen" while their sound and stage performance is largely classic heavy metal, providing the "wu."
