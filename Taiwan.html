Taiwan

Taiwan, formally the Republic of China, is a major economic power in spite of its small size. It is a very advanced country known especially for its production of microchips and computer technology. Taiwan's history and location, however, are a source of insecurity and tension. Following the Communist Revolution's success in mainland China in 1949, Chinese nationalist forces who had fought against the communists fled to Taiwan, setting up an independent government. Ever since then, mainland China has refused to recognize Taiwan's independence and pledged to unify the two portions of what they consider China leading to persistant tensions and threats. Until 1987, Taiwan was ruled under martial law suppressing political opposition and freedoms in the process. This may have impacted the relatively late start in the emergence of the metal scene in Taiwan. While Taiwan is today democratic with a much more open society tensions with China remain high.





Taiwanese Diaspora Death Metal: Ripped to Shreds

While they are currently headquartered in San Jose, California Ripped to Shreds is very much a band of the Taiwanese diaspora. The band's leader, Andrew Lee, grew up in Taiwan and the common notion that ex-pats maintain a strong connection to their home society is true with his band. While the band's sound is firmly death metal the lyrical themes and content are very much influenced by Chinese mythology and folklore and Lee sings in both English and Mandarin. Songs on their latest release mix Taoist beliefs in the underworld with colorful funeral rites of gangsters in rural Taiwan.
